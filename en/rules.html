BoomBoom Taxi service rules

These rules exist to protect the driver, their car, and their income first. By using BoomBoom Taxi, passenger and driver confirm agreement with the terms below.

1. Role of the service BoomBoom Taxi is a technology platform connecting passengers and drivers. The service is not a transportation company and does not take responsibility for vehicle condition or traffic compliance — the driver is responsible. The trip is provided by the driver in their own car.
2. Passenger responsibility for the car The driver’s car is personal property and can be expensive. Passengers must treat the interior and exterior with care: do not stain, scratch, or break interior elements or equipment; do not damage belts, handles, headrests, or other parts. Any damage must be fully compensated.
3. Food, drinks, and cleanliness During the trip, food or drinks that can spill, smell, or make a mess (coffee, juices, fast food, etc.) are not allowed. The driver may ask to put food/drinks away before the ride or refuse the trip. If cleaning or detailing is needed because of the passenger (spills, dirty shoes on seats, trash), the passenger must cover cleaning costs and any downtime.
4. Traveling with animals Animals are allowed only if they are in a carrier/container or on a protective mat/cover. The driver may refuse the trip if the animal is without a carrier, heavily shedding, wet, dirty, or could damage the interior. If the animal causes mess or damage, the passenger is fully responsible and compensates losses.
5. Driver’s right to refuse a trip The driver may decline an order if the passenger is aggressive, heavily intoxicated, carrying prohibited items, bringing an animal without a carrier, or if there is a risk to the interior or safety. The driver can refuse without detailed explanation if they see risk.
6. Payment and protecting driver income The passenger must pay according to the driver’s rate and service rules. Payment can be cash, card, or cryptocurrency. The driver receives the amount minus platform commission. If payment is refused, the service may block the passenger and assist with debt recovery.
7. Changing the route Passenger and driver may adjust the route by mutual agreement. Cost recalculates automatically by actual distance and time. If the change increases cost, the passenger pays the new amount. The driver may refuse a change if it is unsafe or unprofitable.
8. Passenger behavior and safety Passengers must not act aggressively, insult or threaten the driver, interfere with vehicle control, distract the driver, smoke in the car, or consume alcohol/drugs during the trip. If rules are broken, the driver may end the trip early and drop the passenger off in a safe place.
9. Ratings, complaints, and blocks Driver and passenger rate each other after the trip. If a passenger repeatedly breaks rules, damages the car, refuses payment, or threatens drivers, the service may temporarily or permanently block the account. In disputes, protecting the driver, their property, and working time is the priority.
10. Conflicts and damage compensation In disputes, the driver may provide photos/videos of the car, chat history, route, and other evidence. The service reviews the case and may: support a compensation claim, block the passenger, limit functionality, or pass materials to authorities.
11. Protecting driver personal data The platform does not disclose a driver’s personal data to passengers: home address, personal phone number, documents, etc. Communication and data transfer happen through protected service channels.
12. Updating the rules BoomBoom Taxi may update these rules. The latest version is published on the site. Continuing to use the platform means you accept the new terms.
